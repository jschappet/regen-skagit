The Skagit Valley has been lived with, cared for, and known for thousands of years.

We acknowledge the Upper Skagit, Swinomish, Samish, and Sauk-Suiattle peoples 
and honor their enduring presence, stewardship, and relationship to this land.

Skagit means 'A Place of Refuge'

We commit to listening and learning with respect.

## Our First Step - Mapping

And by mapping we mean, asking:  
- Who is here? 
    - People, animals, plants, organizations... 
- What do we need to live here?
- What are we growing, making, building, tending?
- What are the stories that build the myths of this place?

We will host learning journeys at Childs Creek Farm.

- Reforestation  
- Food forest systems  
- Humans participating in ecological repair  

What does it feel like to reforest land?

## Part of a Larger Bioregion
Regenerate Skagit is part of a pilot cohort within the broader Regenerate Cascadia network.  

Ten teams across our bioregion  
are experimenting with place-based regeneration.  

Each watershed is unique.  

Our focus is here — Skagit.

---

## It is an experiment in cooperation.  

A practice of talking and listening.  

A way to make the invisible web visible.
