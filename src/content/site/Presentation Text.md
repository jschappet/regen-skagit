# Skagit River Valley
-----
## From Snow-Capped Mountains to the Salish Sea
The Skagit River watershed stretches from the glaciers of the North Cascades all the way to the Salish Sea, carrying water, soil, and stories through one of Cascadia’s most life-giving landscapes. 

As one of the largest watersheds in Cascadia, it feeds an alluvial floodplain layered with some of the top 2% of agricultural soils in the world. That rich soil and steady water have sustained communities and food systems for generations. 

-----
## The Indigenous People of the Skagit Valley
This place has been lived with, cared for, and known for thousands of years.

We acknowledge the Upper Skagit, Swinomish, Samish, and Sauk-Suiattle peoples and honor their enduring presence, stewardship, and relationship to this land.

We commit to listening and learning with respect.

https://www.washingtontribes.org/the-tribes-of-washington/

## Why Work on the Bioregional Scale?
Organizing by bioregion makes sense because it aligns our human relationships with the living systems that already sustain us—the water we share, the land we depend on, and the place that shapes us together.

As we attune to the land, relationship deepens. From relationship, belonging emerges.

### Listening to the River Means Paying Attention to This Place

### 2025 Flood
In 2025, the Skagit River overflowed its banks, reminding us that what happens upstream and downstream affects us all.

## From a Small Circle: How Regenerate Skagit Began

### Building a Living Practice
We began in spring 2025 by meeting each Friday and asking each other honest questions.  
Over shared meals, our commonalities became clear.  
Small projects—harvesting and yard clean-up—built trust through action.  
A small circle grew into a living network of practice.

## Regenerate Skagit Taking Root

### OUR VISION
A bioregional culture rooted in the Skagit watershed, where humans live in relationship by attuning with land, water, and each other.  
We listen deeply, imagining together, and remembering that we are of the land that sustains us.

### OUR MISSION
To cultivate bioregional awareness and belonging by inviting people of all ages to see, feel, and imagine life from the perspectives of the watershed—its rivers, trees, animals, farms, and communities. We do this through the practice of deep listening, shared art-making, embodied conversation, and collective attunement to the land and one another.
