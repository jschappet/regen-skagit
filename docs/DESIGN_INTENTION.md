# Regenerate Skagit Website — Design Intent

## Purpose
This website is not a marketing funnel or an advocacy platform.  
It exists to help people **orient themselves** to Regenerate Skagit: what it is, how it works, and what is possible to participate in.

The site should feel more like a **trail sign** than a call to action.

---

## Core Principle: Orientation Over Persuasion
We are not trying to convince visitors to do something.  
We are trying to make what exists **legible** so people can decide for themselves.

Language should:
- Describe rather than demand
- Reveal rather than recruit
- Preserve reader autonomy at every step

Avoid language that implies urgency, obligation, or scarcity.

---

## Mobile-First as an Ethical Choice
Mobile-first is not just a technical decision — it’s a philosophical one.

Design for:
- One thumb
- Limited attention
- People encountering the site in everyday life, not “research mode”

The site should make sense in **two calm swipes**, with depth available through continued scrolling.

Desktop layouts should *expand* mobile clarity, not add complexity.

---

## The Trail Sign Metaphor
A trail sign:
- Names where you are
- Describes the terrain ahead
- Marks different paths without ranking them
- Allows turning back without shame

Our site should do the same.

Buttons and links should function like **path labels**, not commands.

---

## Calls to Action (or Lack Thereof)
We intentionally avoid strong “call to action” language.

Instead of:
- Join now
- Take action
- Sign up

We prefer:
- Ways to participate
- Gatherings & events
- Staying connected
- How this works

Interaction should feel like **curiosity continuing**, not compliance.

---

## Participation Model
Participation in Regenerate Skagit is:
- Invitational, not extractive
- Flexible over time
- Based on consistency, not intensity

The site should never imply:
- Minimum commitments
- Membership requirements
- Escalation after clicking

Every step should be reversible and low-pressure.

---

## Events and Gatherings
Events are shown to **signal rhythm**, not to drive conversion.

Whenever possible:
- Show an upcoming or recent gathering passively
- Allow details to expand gently
- Avoid countdowns, urgency language, or “limited spots” framing

People should feel welcome to observe before participating.

---

## Tone and Aesthetic
The site should feel:
- Calm
- Grounded
- Human-scale
- Spacious rather than dense

Avoid:
- Promotional hype
- Visual noise
- Over-designed components that demand attention

Whitespace and restraint are features, not omissions.

---

## What Success Looks Like
A successful site:
- Helps someone understand Regenerate Skagit in under a minute
- Makes it clear that participation is possible, but not required
- Encourages return visits without pressure
- Feels trustworthy because it doesn’t rush the reader

If someone leaves thinking,  
> “I know what this is, and I could come back,”  
the site is doing its job.

---

## Final Note to Collaborators
This site is an extension of how Regenerate Skagit practices community.

Design decisions should be tested against a simple question:

**Does this help someone orient themselves, or does it try to move them?**

If in doubt, choose orientation.
